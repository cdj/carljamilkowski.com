Actuator Development For Biomimetic Underwater Locomotion

Deliverables

The purpose of this project is to build a mechanical fish that can emulate the movements of a real fish using three degrees of freedom. The mechanical fish will be able to achieve flow across its surface similar to that created by a real fish, including the vertices created by the movements of the tail fin. This necessitates the creation of a fish with a reasonable surface skin.

In designing the fish, animated computer models will be made along with mock-ups in order to ensure the mechanical parts can withstand the forces required of them. Tests or calculations will have to be done in order to estimate the force needed to move a body in static water at the rate of 1 Hz.

The design will allow for the fish to be easily attached to the current joystick setup found in the lab. It will be submersible in water and will be able to fit within the constraints of the pool. The mechanisms of the robot will be controllable through cables running through the joystick that is vertically attached to the robot. The fish must also accommodate a payload of sensors, which are very small, and will be attached later. All of this shall be delivered upon completion on May 5th within the bounds of the budget.
