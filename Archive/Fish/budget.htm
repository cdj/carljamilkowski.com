Actuator Development For Biomimetic Underwater Locomotion

Budget

The $500 granted by the department will serve as a ceiling for the initial prototype of the fish. Depending on what Professor Kelly intends to do with the fish, further expenses may be incurred for controlling the fish in the lab. These expansions beyond the initial scope of the project will be up to Professor Kelly to fund. No travel should be required in order to fulfill design requirements. Access to a machine shop will be required, but because of the limited size of the fish, the machining time should be minimal. The controls required for the fish can already be found in Professor Kelly’s lab and therefore are not included in the budget.
