Actuator Development For Biomimetic Underwater Locomotion

Solution Procedure

To complete the goals and objectives previously described, this team will follow a detailed schedule to remain punctual with deadlines in order to complete a working autonomous robotic fish. The solution procedure is outlined by objectives and planned days until the design is completed. Below is a Gantt chart outlining the procedure dates, followed by detailed descriptions of each step.

Proposal Review (February 15 – February 17)

Following the team’s submission of the project proposal, issues brought up by the faculty will be further discussed and understood to improve upon the project.

Actuator Testing (February 10 – February 20)

One aspect of the project yet to be determined is which method should be used to create the movement of the fish. One method being considered is air muscles (pictured on left), which are controlled by pressure, to create the motion in a system of links. The other method is to use electrically controlled servo motors to create the fish-like motion. Both methods will be tested on similar links and compared to decide which method returns the best results as far as accuracy, quickness, reliability, and compatibility with a control system.

Linkage Design and Test (February 15 – February 28)

The linkages used in the robot must display smooth motion along with ease of use for the chosen actuator. The linkage design must also allow a full range of desired positions within a thirty degree limit. For these reasons, several simple linkage designs will be tested and evaluated.

Control System Design (February 21 – March 11)

In order for the robot to advance to several positions a computer control system must be implemented. Depending on the chosen method of actuation, air muscle or servo motor, either an air or electrical control system will have to be designed and placed into the project design.

Joint System Build (March 1 – March 18)

After the most desirable linkage has been found, it will be implemented into a four-link system. This set of links will be used to create the desired three-degree-of-freedom motion for the robot. The chosen actuators will also be assembled onto the joints during this time.

Control System Test with Four Links (March 13 – April 3)

During this time period the three-degree of freedom linkage and control system will be combined and tested together. It is important during this time that the robot system be tested to display accurate aquatic motion along with the desired frequency of motion greater than one hertz.

Design and Build Fish Body (March 27 – April 3)

After the control system and linkages are assembled, a skin can be designed and built around it to guarantee a waterproof robot. It is essential that the tail of the body be flexible enough to still allow the proper frequency of motion.

Final Build (April 4 – April 25)

The final build will consist of putting all the parts of the robot together and finalizing the assembly. This portion will also consist of testing the robot for accurate motion and reliability.
